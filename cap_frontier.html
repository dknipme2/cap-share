Who builds it first

Small teams reach the composition first — and we're inside that window

If five communities are converging on the same composition, the next question is who builds it first. Worth being honest: the frontier is not theoretical for pharma. Anthropic and Novo Nordisk shipped Claude in regulatory documentation — 10+ weeks of work compressed to 10 minutes. Novartis's CEO joined Anthropic's board. Sanofi, AbbVie, and others are in motion. Frontier models are being deployed inside the largest pharma companies right now.

But the scope is narrow. Those deployments are single use cases — regulatory documentation automation, lab workflows, drug-discovery pipelines. The full commercial composition — sizing, S&T, payer access, LOE defense, portfolio review, all running off a shared graph and a closed feedback loop — is still ahead. Even the biggest frontier-lab + big-pharma partnerships haven't gone there yet, because big pharma has to re-map its operating model around agentic work first. That re-map takes years: regulatory governance, established analytics functions, committee culture, change-management processes, stakeholder layers. A small embedded team doesn't carry that weight. We compose now. We ship. We correct. The window closes as the capability scales — which is exactly why this kind of work lands here first.

What we composed

Eight elements: one orchestrator, three that fire per question, two that feed them, one foundation, one feedback loop

If small teams reach the composition first, the next question is what the composition actually looks like. Here is ours. Agentic orchestration (01) is the spine — what binds the whole. Three pillars fire per question (active). Two feed them (substrate). One supports everything (foundation). One loops the learning back so the next engagement starts further along.

01 · CENTER · AGENTIC ORCHESTRATION

The composition spine

What plans, delegates, and composes across the 7 pillars. The thing Deloitte and McKinsey are pointing at when they say agentic AI. The version that lives in CAP is production-grade, domain-scoped, and disciplined — not a demo.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Anthropic · Building effective agents (2024) — orchestrator-worker, evaluator-optimizer, routing patterns. Deloitte · State of Generative AI in the Enterprise (2024–25). McKinsey · Why agents are the next frontier of generative AI (2024). Gartner · Top Strategic Tech Trends 2025 · Agentic AI.

Ships today

11 specialist roles, triad composition (challenger + architect + strategist), subagent filing-clerk output contract, scoped dispatch per decision class.

Horizon

Measured-autonomy expansion at increasing decision scope, with human gates at decision boundaries.

02 · ACTIVE · MEMORY

Persistent context across sessions

Most LLM work starts fresh every chat. A commercial exocortex cannot. Last month's decisions, this quarter's tuning, yesterday's roundtable feedback — all available on next session start, not reconstructed.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Clark & Chalmers · The Extended Mind (1998) — cognition as a process that includes external scaffolding. Packer et al. · MemGPT / Letta (arXiv 2023) — hierarchical memory tiers beyond the context window. Mem0; LangMem. Tulving's episodic/semantic distinction underwrites the typing model.

Ships today

Auto-memory system (user / feedback / project / reference), append-only session YAMLs loaded at session start, heuristic register as intermediate-term store, typed graph as long-term.

Horizon

Cross-engagement episodic recall — the system surfacing analogous prior-engagement contexts automatically.

03 · ACTIVE · SPECIALISTS

11 specialist roles, composable

Each specialist — strategist, operator, researcher, challenger, architect, analyst, facilitator, groundskeeper, dataops, finops, tester — is a separate role with its own job, methods, and quality bar. The orchestration center decides which to call when, and how to combine them. Roles are composed per question, not hard-wired into a script.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Anthropic · orchestrator-worker & routing patterns. Multi-agent system literature (Wooldridge, Introduction to MultiAgent Systems). Division-of-labor heritage from organizational theory (Smith, Simon). Dispatch patterns from Erlang / actor-model concurrency.

Ships today

11 named role skills, triad review for consequential decisions, parallel subagent dispatch, filing-clerk output contract (parent doesn't edit specialist output).

Horizon

Autonomous multi-step execution within a specialist's scope, with human gates at decision boundaries.

04 · ACTIVE · REASONING

Structured thinking, not chain-of-thought theater

Decision trees route the question to the right method. The method constrains what counts as a good answer. The output is structured answer-first, with the supporting reasoning underneath. An adversarial reviewer stress-tests the claim the recommendation depends on most. Reasoning is infrastructure, not vibes.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Minto · The Pyramid Principle (1987) — SCQA, MECE, answer-first structure. Klein · Sources of Power (1998) — Recognition-Primed Decision. Simon's bounded rationality (A Behavioral Model of Rational Choice, 1955). Klein · Performing a Project Premortem (HBR 2007) underwrites the challenger pattern.

Ships today

Hypothesis-ontology decision tree, method YAMLs constraining analysis, SCQA + evidence chain in deliverables, adversarial triad review.

Horizon

Multi-hop causal reasoning across the graph (e.g., "trace this outcome back to its decision, its finding, its data source, its assumption").

05 · SUBSTRATE · EXPERTISE

Typed graph + domain knowledge base

What the active layer reaches for. Not a search index dumped into a vector store — 21 typed entity kinds with stable IDs (organizations, products, decisions, findings, and the rest), with three-stage retrieval that ranks the right results to the top. Includes a loop that turns field-force roundtable signal — what the reps know that the data doesn't show — into validated, queryable findings.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Nonaka · SECI model / Knowledge-Creating Company (1995) — tacit-to-explicit conversion. Schön · The Reflective Practitioner (1983). Knowledge-graph / ontology tradition: Spotify Backstage, schema.org, UMLS. Lewis et al. · Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks (2020). Nogueira & Cho · Passage Re-ranking with BERT (2019).

Ships today

21 typed entity kinds in the graph, hybrid retrieval + cross-encoder rerank (rerank is load-bearing — disabling it degrades eval), per-entity research configs, tacit-knowledge capture via field-force roundtables → typed findings → validated against silver-layer data.

Horizon

Cross-engagement analog surfacing — the graph automatically identifying prior similar situations across the company's history.

06 · SUBSTRATE · TOOLS / METHODS

Patterns and tools, composable

Two layers. The architect picks the design pattern — what shape the data takes. The operator picks the tool that implements it. Methods sit in a vetted library; the analyst doesn't pick by gut. A capability-matcher checks whether the available data supports the method before the analysis runs. Composable, swappable, extendable without rebuild.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Unix philosophy · patterns literature (Fowler et al.). Snodgrass · bitemporal modeling for the data design toolbox. FAIR data principles (Wilkinson et al. 2016) for provenance. OPA / Rego for policy-as-code. Feasibility matching heritage from method-selection literature in OR / decision analysis.

Ships today

Data design toolbox (12 patterns, architect-facing, domain-agnostic) + data tools toolbox (5 slots, operator-facing, compute-swap surface). Method YAMLs at compute/configs/methods/. Feasibility engine matching analytical roles × grain.

Horizon

Auto-extending toolbox from usage telemetry — the system proposing new tool/pattern slots when engagement traffic suggests them.

07 · FOUNDATION · PLATFORM

The substrate that doesn't bend

Everything above runs on this. Git for versioning, branching, and audit trail. Databricks and Unity Catalog for data. A layered rule system that goes from session-level discipline up to mechanical guardrails enforced at commit time. A 7-tier confidence system that flags every claim as fact, structural, directional, computed, or unverified. Time-stamped fact lineage so we can answer what did we believe when.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

OPA / Rego — policy-as-code. Snodgrass · bitemporal temporal databases. FAIR / PROV-O provenance standards. Unix + Git heritage for version-as-truth. MYCIN-era expert systems for knowledge-acquisition / rule-firing patterns (direct ancestor of the L1–L7 rule stack).

Ships today

Databricks + Unity Catalog + git as the canonical stack. L1 harness → L2 behavioral → L3 operating+domain → L5 role skills → L7 hooks. Pre-commit hook suite for schema, provenance, vendor-scrub, roundtrip integrity. 7-tier confidence-flag system.

Horizon

Multi-tenant isolation, enterprise audit service, SSO + compliance posture. See the honest assessment for the gap list.

08 · THE FEEDBACK LOOP · EVOLUTION

The system gets sharper with use

Every engagement leaves signal — what worked, what failed, what surprised. That signal flows back into the system as a captured note, then a candidate rule, then (if it holds up to peer review) a durable part of how the system runs the next engagement. The system is where the lesson lives — not in someone's head, not in a deck, not in last quarter's deck. Most peers in the market don't have this loop closed. We do.

Research lineage + what ships today
Lineage

Expert-systems-era knowledge acquisition — MYCIN (Shortliffe 1976) and the rule-acquisition literature that followed. Nonaka · SECI cycle — tacit → explicit → combined → internalized. Klein · pre-mortem for adversarial review patterns. Kahneman & Klein · adversarial collaboration. Virtue epistemology on how reasoning communities improve over time.

Ships today

Engagement learnings are captured automatically and reviewed by a three-perspective panel. Promoted learnings become rules in the system; where the rule can be checked mechanically, a paired guardrail enforces it on the next commit. See Why CAP for the layered stack and graduation story.

Horizon

Promotion triggered automatically when the same learning fires across enough engagements. Less analyst overhead; more learning per cycle.

How to know it's not vibes

A frontier framing is easy to do badly — too aspirational, not enough receipts. Three orthogonal anchors keep this one honest. Market validation — the big shops are converging on the same composition, so the frontier is real, not invented. Research lineage — every pillar has prior art that predates the market label. Production reality — the loop is shipping, not on a roadmap.
