Core Insight — World 1 or World 2

The commercial analytics approach for Cycle's ORMALVI cluster depends entirely on a single data-visibility question: does LeMed (ORMALVI's exclusive SP) report dispensing data to commercial Rx panels (IQVIA Xponent, DDD)? If yes, this is a classic named-account competitive grid with retail-style analytics. If no, Cycle Vita hub data becomes the only usable signal and the entire playbook changes. The answer is binary. The cost of designing analytics without knowing the answer is building for the wrong world.

Strategic Framing

Periodic paralysis is a textbook named-account rare-disease market. The universe is small enough for Cycle's ~29-rep US sales force to enumerate every prescriber, every Center of Excellence, and every patient-advocacy node. A single FDA-approved molecule (dichlorphenamide) is marketed in two commercial forms — the brand originator Keveyis (Xeris) and Cycle's ORMALVI branded generic built on the same ANDA. Both sit on exclusive-SP rails, both carry $0-copay programs, and neither competes on price. The competitive surface is hub services (Cycle Vita vs. Xeris hub) and specialty-pharmacy integration (LeMed vs. Xeris SP), not formulary.

That makes the cluster a Tier-2 candidate in Cycle's portfolio ROI framework — high analytical value if LeMed reporting resolves favorably, low value if it does not. Both outcomes are knowable with a single phone call to IQVIA. Everything downstream is gated on that answer.

Data Gate — The Single Most Important Question

Critical Question

Does LeMed (Cycle's exclusive ORMALVI SP) report dispense data to IQVIA Xponent or Symphony DDD?

LeMed is an independent rare-disease specialty pharmacy. Independent SPs have historically had lower IQVIA reporting compliance than national chains. Xeris's SP for Keveyis is a larger independent rare-disease pharmacy and is more likely to report. If LeMed does not report, ORMALVI's prescriptions are invisible in syndicated commercial data — and all inferences about the competitive grid must come from Cycle Vita hub data alone.

Why the answer can't be assumed

  • Xponent retail coverage is ~93%, but specialty-pharmacy coverage varies by agreement. Exclusive-SP distribution is a known blind spot.
  • Xeris's Keveyis SP is a large independent rare-disease pharmacy — likely reports, but Cycle should confirm.
  • LeMed (Cycle's ORMALVI SP) — reporting status is not publicly disclosed in LeMed's materials.
  • Aggregator coverage varies by contract. IQVIA and Symphony can confirm status directly for a nominal fee; no amount of secondary research answers it.

Bottom line

Claims visibility is gate-dependent — everything hinges on whether LeMed reports to commercial Rx panels. With “yes,” a Tier-1 named-account analytical program becomes executable for Cycle. With “no,” the cluster analytics is speculative until Cycle Vita hub or direct LeMed data is licensed. The first analytical deliverable should be the gate answer itself — not a market assessment.

Analytical Playbook A — If SP Reports

In this world, the ORMALVI cluster becomes a named-account flywheel with full retail-style competitive visibility. The ~100–250 active prescribers are individually enumerable, and every competitive Rx (ORMALVI vs. Keveyis) is visible at prescriber level.

1 · Named-Account Competitive Grid

Prescriber-level head-to-head Rx share (ORMALVI vs. Keveyis) across all enumerable PP prescribers.

  • Every dichlorphenamide-prescribing physician, by volume and Rx share
  • Switching patterns — who is defending vs. who is converting
  • Geographic concentration vs. field-force coverage
  • Academic vs. community prescriber segmentation

2 · Competitor Underinvestment Detection

Xeris Biopharma is publicly focused on a different growth asset (Recorlev for Cushing's). Keveyis is described as "flattening." If Xeris call patterns drop and Keveyis Rx share decays in parallel, that is a named-account opportunity map for ORMALVI.

  • Open Payments time-series — rep interaction frequency by prescriber
  • Rx trajectory by prescriber vs. interaction trend
  • Territory-level field-force coverage gaps

3 · DAW / Substitution Defense

ORMALVI is priced at or above brand. Without DAW (Dispense As Written), PBMs may substitute to unbranded generic dichlorphenamide at a fraction of the price. DAW compliance is existential for ORMALVI economics.

  • DAW-flag rate by prescriber and territory
  • Substitution frequency at pharmacy level
  • Prescriber education ROI — DAW rate delta pre/post intervention

4 · Referral Network Topology

The patient journey runs PCP → general neurology → neuromuscular subspecialty. Referral patterns across this chain are visible in claims and drive Centers of Excellence analytics.

  • Referral-chain maps from general neurology to neuromuscular academics
  • Center-of-Excellence dominance by geography
  • Diagnostic-odyssey length by referral path

Analytical Playbook B — If SP Does Not Report

In this world, commercial Rx panels are blind to ORMALVI. Cycle Vita hub data becomes the sole source of prescribing intelligence — and, as it happens, a richer one for named-account rare disease than syndicated data ever is. The analytical frame shifts from "competitive market share" to "Cycle-direct patient journey."

1 · Cycle Vita Flywheel KPIs

The Cycle Vita hub sees every enrolled ORMALVI patient end-to-end. For a <3,000-patient population, patient-level analytics are the only analytics that matter.

  • FIND: enrollment volume by referral source, diagnostic odyssey length
  • ACCESS: BV → PA → dispense funnel; payer-level approval/denial
  • START: time-to-first-fill (TTFF), bridge-to-commercial conversion
  • MAINTAIN: patient-level refill gaps, discontinuation reasons
  • MEASURE: funnel-stage conversion trending; cost-per-patient-started

2 · Patient Journey Analytics

Cycle Vita enrollment forms carry referral source, diagnosis date, referring specialty, and geography. The Cycle Vita CRM is the only place where diagnostic odyssey length for ORMALVI patients is directly measurable.

  • Attack history → first neurology visit → genetic testing → diagnosis timeline
  • Referral-source mix (rheumatology, cardiology, urology — the "finding" paths)
  • Diagnostic-delay segmentation by geography and patient demographics

3 · Payer Access Analytics

Every ORMALVI PA submission, approval, denial, and appeal flows through Cycle Vita. For rare disease, payer-by-payer PA dynamics drive commercial outcomes more than prescriber targeting.

  • PA approval rate by payer
  • Appeal outcome timelines
  • Bridge-program utilization as a leading indicator of access friction

4 · Nurse-Touchpoint / Service-Tier ROI

Cycle Vita hub services (nutrition, adherence, clinical support) are the only differentiator for ORMALVI against Keveyis and unbranded generic. Attributing retention to specific service tiers is the closest analog to ROI measurement in this world.

  • Retention delta between high-touch and low-touch enrolled patients
  • Correlation of nutrition-program participation with persistence
  • Service-tier cost vs. retention value

Open Questions

  1. Does LeMed report ORMALVI dispensing to IQVIA Xponent and/or Symphony DDD? (Binary data gate.)
  2. Does Xeris's Keveyis SP report, and with what latency? Determines whether competitive Rx intelligence is visible.
  3. Is Xeris actually underinvesting in Keveyis? Xeris commentary suggests yes; Rx-level data would confirm, informing Cycle's ORMALVI growth case.
  4. How concentrated is the prescriber universe? If <50 physicians account for >70% of volume, named-account analytics dominate Cycle's territory alignment for its ~29-rep force.
  5. What is the DAW compliance rate at LeMed? Substitution to unbranded generic dichlorphenamide destroys ORMALVI economics silently.
  6. Can Cycle Vita negotiate direct data feeds from LeMed into a Databricks analytics layer? If LeMed reporting is a no, this becomes the only path.
