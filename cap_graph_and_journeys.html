Worked Example · Field-Force Sizing

One concrete commercial question traced end-to-end through the map above. A launch team asks: "what field-force size supports our target reach and frequency at launch?" Here is what the system does.

exampleFrom question to deployed FF plan

primary roles: strategist · researcher · operator · analyst · dataops · challenger

Workload-buildup method chosen via the hypothesis ontology decision tree. Bronze → silver → gold on Databricks. Full audit trail back to the graph.

01 · frame
Problem definition
Strategist decomposes: launch vs in-line · specialty vs primary-care · single product vs portfolio. The HYPOTHESIS_ONTOLOGY_DECISION_TREE routes to the method family.
HYPOTHESIS_ONTOLOGY_DECISION_TREE·hypothesis
02 · research
Entity research
Researcher dispatches against the per-entity configs: organization (launch co), product (asset), therapy_area, market, event (approval / launch window), data_source (available feeds). KB pre-check surfaces existing RB_*.md briefs before re-research.
organization·product·market
03 · find data
Feasibility check
data_source entities enumerate analytical_roles × grain × geographic_scope. tools/feasibility_check.py matches capabilities against the selected method's requirements. Tier: full / partial / directional.
data_source·feasibility_check.py
04 · bronze→silver
Build the pipe
Operator + dataops land raw ingests in cdp_dev.*_bronze, then clean + conform + enrich into cdp_dev.*_silver with entity IDs (NPIs, product codes, geographic anchors). Unity Catalog governance tags applied per TAG_TAXONOMY.
operator·dataops·Databricks dev
05 · scope method
Select method
Method YAML loaded from compute/configs/methods/. For FF sizing: workload-buildup vs affordable-coverage vs response-informed-AC. Method's data requirements cross-checked against feasibility tier from step 03.
methodology·method YAML
06 · analyze
Run the method
Analyst executes on silver tables: market sizing × adoption curve × call intensity → workload → FF size under reach/frequency targets. Sensitivity analysis on key inputs (adoption, call productivity, territory geometry).
finding·hypothesis updated
07 · silver→gold
Productize outputs
Operator writes productized gold tables: FF size recommendation, territory map, rep allocation, call plan skeleton. Promotion workflow lands in cdp.*_gold (prod, read-only). Refresh cadence set; schema locked.
cdp.*_gold·data_pipeline
08 · review + deliver
Challenge + ship
Challenger auto-dispatches on the "easy" predictions (rep-ratio rules of thumb) for independent verification. Strategist translates findings into a recommendation with SCQA + evidence chain. Deliverables: brief · slide deck · interactive HTML.
challenger·recommendation
09 · save
KB + graph persist
RB brief files to knowledge/research/; entity YAMLs (finding, recommendation, decision, outcome) commit to knowledge/graph/entities/. Novel patterns surface as heuristic candidates. Next FF-sizing engagement starts from this state, not zero.
decision·outcome·facilitator
Databricks · why three tiers
Bronze raw ingests · silver cleaned + joined with entity IDs · gold productized outputs. Analyst works freely in dev; promotion to prod gates governance, schema, refresh cadence. Not a data swamp — a data product.
Method selection · no ad-hoc picks
The decision tree classifies the question — launch vs in-line · specialty vs primary · portfolio vs single-asset — and routes to the vetted method YAML. Every method choice is a traced decision, not an analyst's gut call.
Compounding · cheaper with use
Each engagement leaves an asset trail — entities populated, briefs filed, method tuned, gold tables registered. Engagement 1 starts at step 01. Engagement 5 starts at step 06. The capability gets cheaper the more you use it.
