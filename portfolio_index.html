Claims Data Reality Check

Before the analytical plan, the epistemic floor: what commercial and claims data can actually identify for each product. Five of the ten products have specific, unambiguous ICD-10 codes and retail- or specialty-visible distribution — that is where claims-based analytics delivers on its own. Three depend on SP reporting or hub data for meaningful visibility; a favorable answer unlocks Tier 1 ROI, an unfavorable answer requires licensed data before investment returns. One product (alkaptonuria) sits in a shared-bucket ICD that makes claims-based patient finding structurally impossible — EHR note-NLP and genotype lists fill the gap. One (galactosemia) is pre-approval, where claims validity is prospective. Per-product detail is surfaced on each page under “Claims Data Validity,” and the portfolio-wide comparison lives in ROI Priorities → Claims Data Validity Profile.
