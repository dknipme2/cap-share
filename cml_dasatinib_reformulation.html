PHYRAGO (Dasatinib ASD) — CML PPI-Compatible Reformulation

PHYRAGO (dasatinib amorphous solid dispersion) is a 505(b)(2) NDA-approved reformulation of dasatinib from Cycle Pharmaceuticals, differentiated by bioavailability that is preserved under gastric acid suppression. Independent pricing; distinct NDC; not an AB-rated generic of Sprycel — a regulatory pathway that creates its own formulary and reimbursement category. Launched October 2025.

Commercial thesis in one line

PHYRAGO is the only dasatinib that keeps working when the patient is on a PPI or H2RA — and about one in three TKI-treated CML patients is. The clinical differentiation is real; the commercial window is narrow (Dasynoc PDUFA Aug 2026); the analytical priority for Cycle Pharmaceuticals is identifying the PPI-niche at prescriber and institution level fast enough to drive pre-competitor formulary landings via Onco360.

Disease & Epidemiology

Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML) is a myeloproliferative neoplasm driven by the BCR-ABL1 fusion oncogene, the product of a reciprocal translocation between chromosomes 9 and 22 (the Philadelphia chromosome). BCR-ABL1 encodes a constitutively active tyrosine kinase that drives clonal proliferation of myeloid cells. Untreated, CML progresses through chronic phase (CP) → accelerated phase → blast crisis, which is rapidly fatal. Tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) have transformed CML from a terminal disease to a chronic condition — annual mortality dropped from 10–20% pre-imatinib to ~1% today.

Clinical profile

  • Median age at diagnosis: ~64 years (SEER)
  • Presentation: fatigue, weight loss, night sweats, splenomegaly; often incidentally on CBC
  • Diagnosis: BCR-ABL1 PCR (peripheral blood) + bone marrow biopsy with cytogenetics
  • Treatment phases: chronic → accelerated → blast crisis; ~90% of incident cases are chronic phase
  • Monitoring: quantitative BCR-ABL1 PCR (IS scale); MR3 / MR4 / MR4.5 milestones
  • Life expectancy on TKI: approaching that of age-matched controls in well-managed patients

Patient Journey

CML follows a relatively stereotyped journey compared with most rare diseases — symptom onset is often subtle, but once a CBC abnormality appears the diagnostic pathway is fast and well-standardized.

Pre-diagnosis
Subtle systemic symptoms
Fatigue, unintentional weight loss, early satiety from splenomegaly, night sweats. Primary care or episodic visit; may be dismissed as nonspecific.
Index CBC
Incidental leukocytosis
CBC on a routine visit shows elevated WBC with left-shift and basophilia / eosinophilia. Thrombocytosis common. Triggers hematology referral.
Diagnosis
BCR-ABL1 PCR + bone marrow
Quantitative PCR confirms BCR-ABL1 fusion transcript; bone marrow biopsy with cytogenetics characterizes phase and karyotype. Baseline MRI / molecular response benchmarks set.
First-line TKI
Imatinib or 2G TKI (dasatinib / nilotinib / bosutinib) or asciminib
Guidelines (v1.2026) recommend all approved 1L TKIs. Choice is driven by comorbidities, risk score, patient preference, and side-effect profile. Dasatinib preferred for higher-risk scores, intolerance to imatinib, or CNS involvement.
3 / 6 / 12 months
Molecular response milestones
PCR monitoring at 3 months (BCR-ABL <10%), 6 months (<1%), 12 months (MR3 / MMR). Failure to meet milestones triggers switch.
Switch scenarios
Intolerance or inadequate response
Common switch patterns: imatinib → 2G TKI; 2G → alternate 2G or Scemblix; 2G → ponatinib if T315I mutation. PPI / H2RA co-medication is a key factor for dasatinib patients specifically.
Long-term
Deep molecular response → treatment-free-remission candidacy
MR4.5 sustained >2 years with >3 yrs total TKI exposure makes a patient eligible for a TFR attempt. Dasatinib-specific TFR rates: ~54% at 1 year (1L), ~43% at 1 year (2L+); DASFREE 5-year data supports durability.

Prescriber Universe

CML is managed almost exclusively by hematology-oncologists. The universe is large nationally (~14,500 heme-onc MDs billing Medicare) but the CML-active subset is a concentrated fraction of that — CML is a niche within heme-onc, and not every heme-onc physician actively manages it.

Site-of-care split: community oncology accounts for ~55–60% of CML care; academic medical centers ~30–35%; hospital outpatient ~10–15% (overlapping). Treatment concentration is highest at NCI-designated cancer centers and large integrated delivery networks.

Claims-Based Identification Opportunities

Unlike ultra-rare diseases that need EHR / NLP or hub data, CML is visible in public claims. Part D captures every oral TKI fill at NDC granularity; Part B captures BCR-ABL PCR ordering. The PPI-niche is identifiable by claims overlap.

1 · Part D TKI Utilization — The Dasatinib Universe

CMS Part D prescriber file captures NDC-level prescribing at provider granularity. Dasatinib — branded, reformulation, and generic — each has its own NDC and each is separately identifiable.

  • Dasatinib NDCs — reference (Sprycel), PHYRAGO ASD reformulation, and generic ANDA products
  • Other TKI NDCs — imatinib (generic), nilotinib (Tasigna), bosutinib (Bosulif), ponatinib (Iclusig)
  • Asciminib (Scemblix) NDC — STAMP inhibitor, primary 1L competitor
  • Total annual dasatinib claims per prescriber (volume decile)

Expected yield: complete prescriber-level map of the dasatinib universe; refresh lag ~18 months. Supplemented by Xponent / Symphony for near-real-time Rx data if subscribed.

2 · Part B BCR-ABL PCR Frequency — The CML-Active Signal

BCR-ABL1 quantitative PCR is billed under Part B. Prescribers with high PCR ordering volume are actively monitoring CML patients — a complementary signal to Part D TKI prescribing.

  • BCR-ABL1 quantitative PCR (molecular monitoring): CPT 81206 (major BCR-ABL, p210), CPT 81207 (minor, p190), CPT 81208 (other breakpoints)
  • Bone marrow biopsy and cytogenetics codes (diagnostic, baseline)
  • ABL kinase mutation testing codes (switch decision support)

Expected yield: identifies providers actively managing molecular monitoring. Cross-referenced with Part D TKI prescribing, isolates providers running the full CML longitudinal model — the targeting tier.

3 · PPI / H2RA Co-Prescribing — The Niche Signal

The heart of the commercial thesis. At patient level, claims overlap between dasatinib Rx and concurrent PPI or H2RA Rx isolates the addressable niche. At prescriber level, the fraction of dasatinib patients with PPI co-medication yields per-prescriber PPI-niche exposure.

  • PPI NDCs — omeprazole, esomeprazole, pantoprazole, lansoprazole, rabeprazole, dexlansoprazole
  • H2RA NDCs — famotidine, cimetidine, nizatidine
  • Dasatinib concurrent-fill overlap — defines patient-level niche membership
  • Prescriber-level PPI-niche prevalence — fraction of dasatinib patients on acid suppression
  • Age-stratified PPI prevalence — 65+ has higher background PPI use

Expected yield: the PHYRAGO PPI-niche sizing answer. ~33% aggregate prevalence means roughly 6,600–9,900 US dasatinib patients have concurrent acid suppression — the addressable market ceiling.

4 · Open Payments + NPI Facility Mapping — KOL and Institutional Targeting

Historical dasatinib-related payments from Bristol Myers Squibb (Sprycel) identify already-warm physicians — speakers, advisors, investigators. NPI facility affiliation maps prescribers to treatment centers for institutional pull-through of PHYRAGO.

  • CMS Open Payments — dasatinib-specific payments (speaker, consulting, research, travel)
  • Aggregate payments by institution — KOL density by academic medical center
  • NPI facility affiliation — map prescribers to treatment centers for institutional targeting
  • Top-100 CML treatment centers by dasatinib claim volume

Expected yield: ranked KOL list for medical-affairs engagement; ranked institution list for access and formulary pull-through. Primary KOLs include Hagop Kantarjian (MD Anderson), Jorge Cortes (Georgia Cancer Center), Michael Mauro (MSKCC), Jerald Radich (Fred Hutchinson), Elias Jabbour (MD Anderson).

Time-pressure callout

The competing pH-independent dasatinib (Dasynoc, Xspray Pharma) has a PDUFA date approximately six months from the publication of this page. A material delay or CRL extends the PHYRAGO incumbent-advantage window; an on-schedule approval creates a two-entrant pH-independent niche that splits share. All analytical work on PHYRAGO is time-pressured — analysis dated after the competitor decision is retrospective rather than strategic. Prioritize analyses that inform institutional and KOL landing decisions before mid-2026 over work that optimizes against a steady-state market.

Open Questions

  1. First 6 months launch trajectory. PHYRAGO new starts vs switches from Sprycel; PPI-niche capture; institutional vs community split.
  2. PHYRAGO formulary coverage. Preferred, non-preferred, step-edit behind generic dasatinib, or not covered. Major PBM and Part D plan coverage status.
  3. Institutional pull-through data. Which top-100 CML treatment centers have added PHYRAGO to formulary; which have declined in favor of generic; which are under P&T review.
  4. PHYRAGO WAC pricing. Not publicly disclosed; essential for commercial modeling and payer access posture. Premium to generic, discount to Sprycel — exact position matters for payer negotiation.
  5. HCPCS / J-code status. Whether CMS has assigned a unique code for PHYRAGO — drives reimbursement pathway distinctness from the 505(b)(2) NDA.
  6. Positioning vs Scemblix in 1L. Is PHYRAGO positioned as 1L-preferred for the PPI-subset, or primarily 2L+? Drives share ceiling.
  7. Dasynoc PDUFA contingency plan. Pricing, access, and messaging moves if the competitor is approved on schedule.
  8. Low-dose dasatinib trend. PHYRAGO ASD formulation support for 70 mg dosing while preserving PPI compatibility — incremental differentiation potential.
