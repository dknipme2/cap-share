Three visual options for the 8-element composition

Same content, three compositions. 1 center (Agentic orchestration) + 7 pillars (Memory · Specialists · Reasoning · Expertise · Tools / Methods · Platform · Evolution). Below: each rendered so we can compare at a glance and close on one.

OPT · ALayered architecture

strategist's lean

Agentic orchestration is the crown that binds. Active layer (what fires per question) sits above substrate (what it reaches for), sits above foundation. Evolution is the loop that feeds the next engagement back in.

Read: the crown Agentic is the composition spine. Reader sees the architecture — active over substrate over foundation — and sees Evolution as the loop wrapping the right edge. Most intellectually honest composition. Risk: densest of the three; could overwhelm a first-time reader.

OPT · BHub and orbit

most striking

Agentic orchestration at the exact center. 7 pillars arranged as satellites. One composition, one image, one gestalt.

Read: one composition, one image. Eye lands on the center; the 7 satellites are peer contributors. Most striking visually; easiest to screenshot for a board slide. Risk: flattens architecture — Platform (foundation) and Memory (active) read as equivalent peers, which they aren't. If the reader looks once and moves on, they'll read "CAP has 7 things around a core" and miss the layering story.

OPT · CPeriodic-table grid

fastest to scan

8 tiles in a clean grid — agentic center marked as a distinct card (bold border / teal fill), the other 7 arranged around it in reading order. No relational arrows; the page text carries the architecture claim.

Read: fastest to scan. Works on a business-deck slide at thumbnail size. Color-codes the role type (center / active / substrate / foundation / loop) without relying on arrows. Risk: flat — an inattentive reader will interpret all 8 tiles as peers and miss that Platform is foundation vs. Memory is active. The supporting page text has to carry that claim.

How I'd choose

If the page is read slowly — for a research-curious leader spending 3+ minutes — Option A. The architecture argument is the point, and A carries it structurally. Most defensible under a second look.

If the page is ALSO a screenshot — something your boss will pull into a board deck or Slack — Option B. One composition, one image, instantly legible. Loses the layering claim but gains the gestalt.

If the constraint is speed — a reader who'll spend 30 seconds — Option C. Legend-driven. Carries the role-type coding without requiring arrows or a read-order.

My open lean is A, with B as the "embedded screenshot" alternate. C is the fallback if the page needs to be printable / hand-outable. We could also ship A on the page + B as the downloadable share asset.
