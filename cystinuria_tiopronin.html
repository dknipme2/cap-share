VENXXIVA (Tiopronin) — Cystinuria Claims Analytics

VENXXIVA (tiopronin delayed-release) from Cycle Pharmaceuticals — a cystine-binding thiol agent that prevents cystine-stone formation in adults and pediatric patients with severe homozygous cystinuria. Indicated as an add-on to high fluid intake, urinary alkalinization, and dietary modification in patients who fail conservative therapy alone. A branded generic; tiopronin has been in clinical use since 1988.

Core insight

VENXXIVA has the strongest claims-based substrate in Cycle Pharmaceuticals' portfolio. The diagnosis code (E72.01) is specific, every stone procedure is coded, every tiopronin fill is visible in Part D, and stone-composition analysis yields a lab signal. The commercial challenge for VENXXIVA is not finding patients — it is converting prescribing away from commodity generics of the same molecule (and from Thiola EC, the Travere reference) and converting untreated patients with stone burden onto pharmacotherapy. Vanscoy Rare's exclusive distribution plus Cycle Vita hub services are the primary service differentiators.

Disease & Epidemiology

Cystinuria is an autosomal recessive inherited metabolic disorder caused by mutations in SLC3A1 and SLC7A9, which encode subunits of the renal proximal-tubular amino acid transporter responsible for reabsorbing cystine and the dibasic amino acids ornithine, lysine, and arginine ("COLA"). Defective transport drives excessive urinary cystine excretion; cystine is poorly soluble — especially at acidic pH — and precipitates as hexagonal crystals that aggregate into kidney and ureteral stones. Disease course is recurrent stone formation from childhood through adulthood, with progressive renal damage in severely affected patients.

Clinical hallmarks

  • Recurrent kidney and ureter stones — typically starting in childhood or adolescence
  • Hematuria, flank pain, renal colic — classic acute presentation
  • Cystine crystals in urine (hexagonal, pathognomonic under polarized light)
  • Urinary tract infections secondary to obstruction and instrumentation
  • Progressive renal insufficiency in chronically undertreated patients
  • 2:1 male : female severity skew — males have higher urinary cystine excretion on average

Patient Journey

Most patients present first to emergency care with acute renal colic in childhood or adolescence. The workup is shaped by whether the first stone is analyzed and whether the patient is routed to nephrology for chronic management or left with episodic urology follow-up only.

Childhood / adolescence
First stone — acute presentation
Flank pain, hematuria, dysuria. ER visit. Imaging (CT KUB or ultrasound) identifies stone; urology consulted for intervention if obstructing.
First workup
Stone composition → cystinuria suspected
Cystine stones have a characteristic hexagonal crystal morphology under polarized light. Stone composition analysis is the highest-yield diagnostic cue — if the first stone is analyzed, diagnosis is often made at the index event.
Confirmation
24-hour urine cystine quantification
Values >250 mg/day in a non-pediatric patient confirm treatment indication. Urine pH, sodium, and volume are assessed concurrently to plan conservative management.
Recurrence window
Conservative management trial (fluid, alkali, diet)
Sodium restriction (<2 g/day), low-methionine diet, hydration to >3 L urine/day, potassium citrate to urine pH 7.0–7.5. ~33% of patients are maintained on conservative measures alone.
Escalation
Pharmacotherapy — tiopronin first-line (VENXXIVA / Thiola EC / commodity generic)
If conservative measures fail after ~3 months, or if cystine excretion exceeds ~500 mg/day, first-line thiol agent is tiopronin — dispensed as VENXXIVA (Cycle, via Vanscoy Rare), Thiola EC (Travere reference), or commodity generic; 800 mg/day divided TID, titrated to urine cystine <250 mg/L. D-penicillamine is the second-line alternative for tiopronin-intolerant patients.
Ongoing
Chronic Rx + urology co-management for stone events
Nephrology manages medical therapy; urology handles episodic surgical intervention (ureteroscopy, laser lithotripsy, PCNL). Multidisciplinary clinics cut surgical intervention rates roughly in half.
Adherence challenges
Sulfur-related side effects drive discontinuation
Tiopronin discontinuation rate ~31% driven by nausea, oral ulcers, rash, arthralgia, proteinuria. Patient-support services targeting adherence (Cycle Vita + Vanscoy Rare for VENXXIVA) are the primary commercial differentiator in a commoditized molecule.

Who orders testing and prescribes?

  • Urologists — first point of contact at stone events; order stone composition analysis; perform surgical interventions.
  • Nephrologists — primary ongoing prescribers; monitor 24-hr urine cystine; titrate tiopronin.
  • Pediatric nephrologists — meaningful subset given 80% first-stone-before-20 statistic.
  • Emergency medicine / PCP — entry point but not longitudinal manager.
  • Multidisciplinary stone clinics — co-located nephrology + urology + dietetics; gold-standard model (AUA data: ~50% reduction in surgical intervention rates).

Claims-Based Identification Opportunities

VENXXIVA has the strongest claims substrate in Cycle Pharmaceuticals' rare-disease portfolio. Every step in the journey leaves a code: the diagnosis is specific, the procedures are specific, the labs are orderable, and the Rx is visible at NDC level in Part D. Four complementary approaches, all of which can run without hub data:

1 · Primary ICD-10 Lookup — The Named Cohort

Cystinuria has a billable, highly specific ICD-10 code. This is the single cleanest starting point for any rare-disease claims analysis in the portfolio.

  • Primary: E72.01 — Cystinuria
  • Concurrent stone diagnoses: N20.0 kidney stone, N20.1 ureter stone, N20.2 both, N21.* lower-tract stone
  • Renal insufficiency / CKD secondary to recurrent obstruction: N18.*

Expected yield: high. Unlike alkaptonuria's shared bucket, E72.01 is disease-specific and non-overlapping with adjacent conditions.

2 · Stone-Procedure Code Cascade — The Burden Signal

Stone procedures quantify unmet treatment need. Every procedure on an E72.01-diagnosed patient is prima facie evidence of medical-therapy failure or non-initiation.

  • CPT 50080 / 50081 — Percutaneous nephrolithotomy (PCNL)
  • CPT 50590 — Extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL)
  • CPT 52352 — Ureteroscopy with lithotripsy
  • CPT 52353 — Ureteroscopy with laser lithotripsy
  • CPT 52356 — Ureteroscopy with laser lithotripsy + stent
  • DRGs 691/692 — urinary stones with ESWL (with / without CC/MCC); 693/694 — without ESWL

Expected yield: very high. Providers with high stone-procedure volume on E72.01-diagnosed patients are the highest-priority outreach segment — their patients are surgicating more because medical therapy is failing, absent, or uninitiated.

3 · Laboratory Signals — The Workup Trail

Labs specific to the cystinuria workup surface both diagnosed patients and undiagnosed patients under workup. Useful as a complement to ICD-based cohorting.

  • 24-hour urine cystine quantification (LOINC codes for cystine urinary excretion)
  • Stone composition analysis — panel cascade (cystine, calcium oxalate, uric acid, struvite)
  • Urine pH (concurrent monitoring of alkali therapy adherence)
  • Urinary sodium (adherence proxy for dietary counseling)
  • SLC3A1 / SLC7A9 genetic testing orders (pediatric and ambiguous adults)

Expected yield: high-precision. Patients with repeat 24-hr urine cystine orders are actively being monitored; those with a single order and no follow-through are the highest-value re-engagement targets.

4 · Pharmacy Signals — Brand / Generic / Alternate Splits

Part D captures every tiopronin fill at NDC granularity, separating VENXXIVA from Thiola EC, commodity generics, and the older alternative D-penicillamine. This is the competitive-share dataset.

  • Tiopronin NDCs — VENXXIVA (Cycle branded generic) + commodity generics
  • Thiola, Thiola EC (Travere) — reference brands
  • D-penicillamine (Cuprimine) — second-line thiol; ~69% discontinuation rate drives low share
  • Potassium citrate — alkali therapy marker (all cystinuria patients)
  • Captopril — rare off-label thiol use; weak evidence

Expected yield: NDC-level share visibility across every prescriber in the US with Part D activity. Commercial-plan share requires Xponent or equivalent retail Rx panel.

Market trajectory

Published industry analyses project tiopronin-category revenue declining at roughly -15.8% CAGR through 2038, driven by generic entry (the first AB-rated generics launched in 2024) and the underlying Shkreli/Retrophin pricing-controversy legacy. The commercial play for VENXXIVA is not category growth — it is capturing and retaining share of a shrinking branded pie, with Vanscoy Rare + Cycle Vita services as the moat.
