MS Oral DMTs Cluster — BAFIERTAM + TASCENSO ODT

Cycle Pharmaceuticals' two-product oral cluster inside the multiple sclerosis disease-modifying therapy (DMT) segment: BAFIERTAM (monomethyl fumarate, acquired from Banner Life Sciences in 2024) and TASCENSO ODT (fingolimod ODT). Both products address the same ~7,400 MS neurologist prescriber universe, share the Cycle Vita patient support hub, and face the same competitive dynamic — defending branded share against low-cost generic equivalents. Cluster-level analytics outperform per-product analytics because the Cycle rep carries both and the prescriber writes both.

Core cluster insight

Neither BAFIERTAM nor TASCENSO ODT is growing on its own. The MS DMT market is migrating from oral DMTs toward higher-efficacy anti-CD20 infusions (Ocrevus, Kesimpta, Briumvi), and inside the oral segment, generic dimethyl fumarate and generic fingolimod capsules have destroyed branded economics at the molecule level. Cluster analytics exist to defend a shared Cycle prescriber relationship — the question is not “how do we grow share?” but “which of the ~7,400 neurologists are worth the two-product call from Cycle's ~29 US reps, and what message sequence retains them?”

Strategic Framing — Why Treat as a Cluster

Four structural facts force cluster-level analytics rather than independent product analytics:

1 · Shared prescriber universe

Both products are prescribed by MS-treating neurologists — a well-defined ~7,400-provider universe in which the top decile writes 41% of all DMT prescriptions. The overlap between fumarate-class prescribers and S1P-class prescribers is high because MS neurologists typically carry a multi-class DMT panel. Targeting, call planning, and territory design should be built around the combined basket.

2 · Shared patient-support infrastructure

The Cycle Vita hub supports both BAFIERTAM and TASCENSO ODT. Clinical Education Managers (CEMs) cover both. Copay support, bridge/free-drug programs, first-dose observation services (for TASCENSO ODT), and benefits verification flow through the same Cycle organization. Hub analytics — enrollment, PA approval, bridge utilization, abandonment — are natively cluster-level.

3 · Shared competitive threat

Both products face the same commercial pressure: a molecule-level generic available at 1–5% of branded price. The generic defense playbook is structurally the same for both — identify prescribers at risk of switching, support GI-tolerability or potency-assurance messaging, and secure payer access for documented-need patients. Defense sequencing is cluster logic, not product logic.

4 · Shared methodology

The class→brand adoption funnel — “of the prescribers writing the molecule class, what share has adopted BAFIERTAM or TASCENSO ODT?” — is the primary analytical construct for both products. Non-adopters become the targeting list; adopters become retention priorities. The method is identical; only the class denominator changes (fumarate prescribers for BAFIERTAM, fingolimod prescribers for TASCENSO ODT).

Class→Brand Adoption Funnel — Primary Analytical Construct

The class→brand funnel is not a visualization. It is a targeting workflow. It converts “prescriber segmentation” from an observational exercise into a ranked action list.

01
Define the class denominator
Enumerate all prescribers who wrote the molecule class within a defined window (e.g., any fumarate DMT in trailing 12 months, any fingolimod in trailing 12 months). Source: CMS Part D for the Part D-visible tail; Xponent for full-market visibility.
02
Intersect with brand numerator
Of class-writing prescribers, which ones have written BAFIERTAM (or TASCENSO ODT, per funnel) at least once? Split into adopters (≥1 script) vs. non-adopters (zero scripts). Adopters are retention priorities; non-adopters are Cycle conversion targets.
03
Rank non-adopters by class volume
Volume-weighted prioritization. A non-adopter writing 200 fumarate scripts/yr is a higher-value target than a non-adopter writing 5. This single reorder of the target list typically outperforms generic deciling.
04
Overlay access feasibility
Intersect with payer mix: non-adopters whose dominant payer mix is friendly (no step-edit, open formulary) rank higher than those in hostile payer geographies. Practical targeting respects what the prescriber can actually fill.
05
Monitor adopter retention
For adopters, track Rx trajectory. Early signs of switching-to-generic (declining branded share within their class volume) trigger retention interventions. This is the second half of the funnel — measured monthly.

Why this is the primary construct

Traditional decile segmentation answers “who is a big prescriber?” It does not distinguish “big prescriber who already uses us” from “big prescriber who uses our class but not our brand.” In a defensive branded niche where molecule-level generics destroy price, the second group is the only economically viable targeting universe. The funnel enforces that discipline at the target-list level.

Part D visibility note

Part D captures only the high-volume prescriber tail above CMS suppression thresholds. For low-volume branded products in a generic-dominated class, this can understate true prescriber reach. The class denominator from Part D is directional; Xponent is required for complete class-level coverage.

Open Questions & ROI Gate

The cluster analytical playbook described above is executable on public data (CMS Part D + Open Payments) and on a licensed Xponent panel. Three unknowns determine the ROI ceiling — each has a concrete unlock.

Open questionWhy it mattersUnlock
What are BAFIERTAM and TASCENSO ODT absolute TRx volumes? Volume determines whether statistical segmentation & targeting (S&T) is viable or whether the cluster needs a named-account / flywheel model. If combined cluster TRx is low, decile-based methods break down. Product-level TRx disclosure from Cycle Pharmaceuticals, or Xponent licensing
Is Cycle's ~29-rep field force dedicated MS or shared with other portfolio products? Workload modeling and territory alignment depend on knowing which reps carry BAFIERTAM + TASCENSO ODT and what other call points they cover. Drives the “combined basket” call capacity calculation. Field-force structure disclosure from Cycle Pharmaceuticals
What is the net-of-rebate realized price per branded prescription? PA win-rate ROI requires net price, not WAC. With copay assistance capped at annual limits and payer step-edits pervasive, realized revenue per patient may be materially below list. Gross-to-net disclosure; can be triangulated from aggregated CMS rebate data as a secondary signal
What blocks full analytical value

The single hardest constraint is product-level TRx data access. Xponent provides retail-pharmacy resolution for both products; without it, the class→brand funnel can identify class prescribers but cannot confirm brand adoption status. Every cluster-level analysis downstream (targeting, retention, defense) degrades from “who writes the brand” to “who writes the class.” This is the first ROI gate to clear.

Summary — What the Cluster Delivers

  1. A cluster target list, not two product target lists. One ranked universe of MS neurologists, scored on combined basket value, with message allocation per prescriber.
  2. A defense-oriented funnel, not a growth funnel. Both products live in declining classes against generic-level pricing. The funnel optimizes retention plus selective conversion, not blanket acquisition.
  3. A two-class view of market trajectory. Fumarate class direction, S1P class direction, anti-CD20 pull-through — one dashboard tells the neurologist and the commercial team where the market is moving.
  4. Access-aware targeting. PA win-rate geographies prioritized over theoretical prescriber volume — what can actually be filled beats what could theoretically be written.
  5. Co-detail efficiency. Measurable call-plan lift from carrying both products into a single visit rather than two separate visits.
